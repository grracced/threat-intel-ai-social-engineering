# Research Notes – AI Social Engineering

## Investigation Goal

Understand how AI-powered social engineering attacks work, how attackers use technology(AI) to manipulate victims, the risks these attacks create for organizations, and how businesses can detect and prevent them.

---

## Source 1 – The Hacker News

### Main Idea

AI is making social engineering attacks more convincing, scalable, and difficult to detect.

### Important Findings

- Attackers use AI-generated voices, videos, and chatbots.
- Social engineering attacks focus on manipulating human emotions such as trust, fear, urgency, and authority.
- AI helps attackers launch attacks faster and at larger scale.
- Deepfake technology is now being used in fraud and impersonation attacks.

### Real Example

A finance employee attended a fake video meeting where deepfake versions of company executives convinced the victim to transfer $25 million to attackers.

## Source 2 – Deepfake CEO Voice Scam Article

### Main Idea

AI voice cloning can be used to impersonate executives during business communications.

### Important Findings

- Attackers cloned the voice of a company CEO.
- The victim believed the voice was genuine because it sounded realistic.
- The attack resulted in financial loss.

### Example

A company employee transferred over $243,000 after receiving a fake phone call that sounded like the CEO.

## Source 3 – MITRE ATT&CK Framework

### Main Idea

MITRE ATT&CK helps security professionals classify attacker behaviors and techniques.

### Findings

- T1598 relates to gathering information for attacks.
- T1566.004 relates to voice-based phishing attacks.
- T1566.003 relates to phishing through communication services.
- T1098 relates to account manipulation.
- T1657 relates to financial theft.

### Example

The AI voice scam and deepfake video call scams can be mapped to multiple MITRE ATT&CK techniques.

## Source 4 – CISA Security Guidance

### Main Idea

Organizations should improve awareness and verification procedures to reduce social engineering risks.

### Findings

- Employees should verify unusual requests before taking action.
- Urgent requests involving money or passwords should be treated carefully.
- Security awareness training is important.
- Multi-factor authentication helps reduce account compromise risks.

### Example

CISA warned that threat actors increasingly use AI and social engineering to target organizations and individuals.

## MITRE ATT&CK Mapping Notes

- T1598 – Phishing for Information
  Attackers gather voice samples and personal information to train AI impersonation tools.
- T1566.004 – Spearphishing Voice
  Attackers use cloned voices during phone calls to manipulate victims.
- T1566.003 – Spearphishing via Service
  Fake communication services and messaging platforms may be used to deceive victims.
- T1098 – Account Manipulation
  Threat actors may maintain unauthorized access through compromised communication accounts.
- T1657 – Financial Theft
  The main objective of many AI social engineering attacks is stealing money from victims or organizations.

---

## Prevention Ideas

- Employee cybersecurity awareness training
- Verification procedures for sensitive requests
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Financial approval processes
- Phishing and social engineering simulations
- Monitoring suspicious account activity
- Using secondary communication channels for verification

---

## Detection Notes

- Watch for urgent or unusual requests.
- Verify unexpected financial transactions.
- Pay attention to suspicious communication behavior.
- Monitor for fake or cloned voices during calls.
- Look for inconsistencies in video meetings or communication style.

---

## Who Is At Risk

- Finance employees
- Executives and managers
- Customer support staff
- Organizations using online communication heavily
- Small and medium-sized businesses
- Individuals with publicly available voice or video content

---

## Observations

- AI social engineering attacks are dangerous because they exploit human trust instead of technical vulnerabilities.

- They use Human emotion to decieve human

- As AI tools continue to improve, deepfake scams and impersonation attacks may become more common and more difficult to identify.
